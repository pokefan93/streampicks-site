# Copy Guidelines

## Desired tone

The website copy should feel:
- sharp
- young
- confident
- competitive
- clear
- premium
- creator-native

## Avoid

Avoid:
- corporate agency language
- generic startup buzzwords
- bloated explanations
- stiff phrasing
- sounding too formal or too “safe”
- feature lists with no emotional payoff

## Lean into

Lean into:
- momentum
- heat
- rivalry
- reveals
- ranking
- social pressure
- progression
- teams
- flexing
- creator culture

## Example directional language

Better directions:
- draft creators and climb
- daily reveals
- rivalry, rank, and bragging rights
- catch creators before they blow up
- build your squad
- call your shot
- climb the ladder
- flex the win

Less desirable directions:
- creator performance insights platform
- data-driven engagement ecosystem
- personalized entertainment gamification layer

## Premium language direction

StreamPicks+ should sound like:
- sharper
- smarter
- more competitive
- more elite
- more customizable

It should not sound like:
- administrative
- storage-based
- utility-only
